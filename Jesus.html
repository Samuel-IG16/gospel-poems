Caught Up For Jesus

There was a man - 'Valentine Burke', An old time buglar With Kilt and guns Always ready to hit and run. The same, in prison, read in a paper "How the jailer of philipi got caught" It tells of the amazing work of Christ in Paul and Silas That were jailed for good works As they prayed and sang As the book of truth records The prison were shakened, the door opened by a great earthquake from the Lord God The jailer, thinking, all had escaped Was about to end his life and end it all Paul said with a voice aloud "Do thyself no harm: Here are we all" The jailer amazed, desired the greatest wish "What must I do to be saved" Paul with the annointing of the Holy Ghost said "Believe in the Lord Jesus Christ And thou and thine house shall be saved" At this, Valentine gave himself to Christ Like the jailer of phillipi Like Valentine Burke, I beseech you come to Christ He will develop your personality Improve your proficiency Realise your prospect And increase your prosperity And above all, He will give you eternal life For a man can make or mar his life.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Caught Up For Jesus is an epic poem that narrates the story of a jailer who gave his life to Christ after witnessing the wonders of God in the life of Paul and Silas while they were in prison. The story can be found in the Book of Acts 16:16–40. This poem was written by the author while awaiting admission into tertiary institution.

At The Cross

Our sins deserve eternal doom But christ for us went to the cross To take on himself our fate and gloom Crucified, no death was so thorough No shame was so complete. To set us free from death, He took this way so rough Stripes and bruises was his body replete With the flogging and scourging post This he did for us with love ultmost. For our iniquity he was bruised and chastised The body became rigid of knotted muscles No gentle massaging hand to see. Colour drained from his lovely cheeks, That were drawn tight to the teeth With cummulative pain no moment of respite Sweat burst from the brow and stung the eyes. The sun shone directly on His beautiful eyes Oh! Jesus my hope; for me offered up. Instead of water they gave him vinegar The macabre scene was played out He humbled Himself, obedient unto death; Unto God the father, he commended His spirit The deed was done; Which shook the earth and veiled the sun. Hallelujah! Up from the grave He arose to reign His death was the end of a foul deed Schemed for; cunningly contrived And above all fiendishly achieved. It is the crown of His sublime deed Planned in Heaven, wrought on earth; And gloriously consummated on cross A glance at the cross saves, the gaze of it sanctifies For now, I feel my Lord's atoning blood Close to my soul applied Me, you He loved - the Son of God His resurrection provides us with power For me, for you He lives to pardon.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

At The Cross is an awesome piece written by the author with the aim of providing insight to the events of the crucifixion of our Lord Jesus Christ — "The purpose of crucifixion" as the author calls it.

Bible references: Matthew 27:26, Luke 23:33, Isaiah 53:3–5, Amos 8:9, Matthew 27:54, John 19:30, 1 Peter 3:18, Philippians 2:5–11, Psalm 16:10, Galatians 2:19–20.

Humility

Humility - the perfection of man's nature Making God to be all The sense of man's nothingness Which comes as seeing God to be all. Rooted in the spirit of lowliness Christ is humilty personified The root from which God's grace grow The only soil in which His grace root And the tree can't do without the root, Is your root pride or humility? Wither do you choose? The ball is in your court Humility is the glory of God's creation The first duty, and highest virtue of creatures. A nobility deeper than contrition Primal to the meek and lowly lamb of God Lack of it is in the root of sin and evil. Old serpent fell from it's glorious pinnacle Breathing the poison of pride to heart of man, Who hitherto fell from his high position. Pride - the gate, births and curses of hell. But the loop-hole is the restoration of humanity Which the meek and lowly lamb did. His humilty is the salvation Expelling all malcontent in saved souls, Lock, stock and barreled against mortal foes; Let this in mind, also.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Humility is a poem that talks about Jesus as the embodiment of humility and the need for us to emulate Him.

Bible references: Proverbs 22:4.

Silent Night

All stars in there full brilliances And the moon in its radiance The night was calm, cool and conducive Suddenly; momentarily the silence was broken Not with the alarm of war or shout of applause; But God's angel praising God, the highest Who to them broke the good tidings; "For unto you is born ...Christ the Lord" It was a remarkable day in annals of human history As the shephard hastened to bade the saviour welcome Oh you sinful and sleepy prodigal' In the silence of your night hear The voice of your conscience and Decide, "I will arise and go to my saviour" That same night the three magicians, Watching on top their tower, The star of God's son held them in consternation Not waiting for the day to dawn They went to worship he who is born king of kings Angels bestowed their ministry in that silent night To who was born of Holy Ghost begotten of God. The night was the expression of God's love The hallmark for his concern for souls. It made all creatures of the earth at peace with joy The night that toll the knell of sin and death, Silently liberating man from throes and thralldom

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Silent Night is an epic poem that narrates the birth of Jesus Christ.

Bible references: Matthew 1:21.

Jesus Christ

God the son whom for His love Came into this world, in form of virgin birth Lived a virtuous life, died a vicarious death And on the third day had a victorious resurrection All to save from hell, man's soul. The testimonies of His coming Are complete, consistent and conclusive His words are axioms, alive with authority He spoke in argument, motive an command Do you understand His arguments? Do you appreciate His motives? Do you obey His commands? Adam brought death, He brings life His love took the place of law and brought joy Gracious with tact to worst sinnners He came as an offering and sacrifice for our sins His death was vicious, official and certified His resurrection is "the best proven fact of history" To liberate us from sin and purchase our pardon By all who appreciate his redemptive work His life is the living epistle to be learned and lived He came to march man's feet and soul, God-ward To this paramount purpose He was whole time, whole man and whole heart In Him is the Way back to God The TRUTH for every bend and road fork The LIFE all along to heaven our home He is the opened door of mercy for the age And a door of hope for the young He is the expression of God's love The hallmark of his concern for souls Call Him into your life And he shall toll the knell of sin and death In your life and above all Make your life soberly, righteously and Godly And eternal life is yours, hereafter.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Jesus Christ is the author's reflection on why Christ came.

Bible references: John 10:10b.

Birth Of Christ

Christ came from His heavenly throne, To make turbulent men, salvation prone So that all and sundry can be His own He was born contrary to the law of life; His love brought Him, a virgin birth for a virteous life The atmosphere damped with sin Could not wet or cling to His skin He was clothed with the garment of righteousness In a stable on a manger as His cradle. A baby calm, cheerful, cool, and collected Content and celebrated For His remarkable disposition Born to stand forth upon the highest pinnacle of heavenly glory His entrance to the stage of the world was pious and Goldly Though He encountered many a cross and difficulty He was all spotless, radiating God's beauty; He was amiable, appproachable and accessible For His blessings which He gave the three magicians and shepherds The shepherd came to bade Him welcome, To us all for righteous life He beckons To see the saviour, the three magicians set on their Way Christ moon arose to lighten their path way; And His bright star directed from the east, their Way The bethlehem baby, is the way and, not a plan or creed, His birth is unique, His purpose is unique The baby unlimited in power, unparalleled in glory Simple as breathing, inevitable as sun rise In infancy He startled king Herod who could not kill Him Man is drugged by sin and satan His birth was the beginning of our liberation The prophecies and testimonies of His birth, Are complete, consistent and conclusive

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Birth Of Christ is an epic poem that narrates the birth of Jesus Christ.

Bible references: Matthew 1:21.

Christ Is Coming

The most thrilling and glorious truth "It is possible any day And imposibble no day" It will dawn and writ large to all A remarkable separation in annals of human A close to a door of extraordinary opportunity And reveals world most despotic dictator Like astronauts journey to space Celestialnaut journey to heaven "For the lord Himself shall descend..." What sober words of inspired reality The saved 'swallows' are soon gone The winter of this world will come "...Caught up together with them in the clouds" What blessed comfort! sweet and precious hope Last book last chapter Penultimate verse and least prayer Testify for His speedy return No good works can jet-propel your soul For it is impossible for wood to be magneted False teachers deny it Religious people delay it Born again Christains desire it What is your attitude towards it? Your decision and choices raise echoes eternal At this stage of time mens heart are filled and failing Life is short, death is sure Sin is the cause, Christ is the cure.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Christ Is Coming is a poem that makes one to be on tip-toe for the rapture.

Bible references: 1 Thessalonians 4:17.

He Came

In this world of hate, lust and greed Where sin is commited to satisfy satan's need His divine dove came and blood In a holy manner and perfect mood God choosen vessel was the virgin mary Who hitherto begat the son, the man of calvary Christ came a ransome for all and sundry To sinners, who by reason of sin were in atmosphere sultry He came to bring order out of chaos So that all in love will sing unto him chorouses; To give beauty out of sordidness; life out of death To this dazed, and bedeviled earth To bring true and enduring gladness In this life devoid of happiness To feel life's cup with joy, His fruit He came to bear witness to the truth, Bitter to palate but sweet in stomach-a perculiar fruit He came to give his life as a ransome for many Who were in the devils company He came to seek and save the lost Which he did with zeal and love utmost He came that we might have abundant life Severing us from sin by his word-sword than knife He came to cast fire on this earth By reason of which he is preparing a better earth Which shall not be dug up for loved ones to be buried As eternity rose on, for we are to him married

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

He Came is an epic poem that narrates the reason for the birth and crucifixion of Jesus Christ.

Bible references: John 6:38, Hebrews 10:7, 1 Timothy 1:15, Hebrews 9:26, John 12:46, Hebrews 2:14–17, John 18:37, Hebrews 2:14, 1 John 3:8, John 6:51, Matthew 2:1–2.

Living In Christ

It is being connected with good living It is everyday, filling up to par A life of exhilirating and ethusiatic journey Through a golden avenue of days For his beloved, humming a happy tune For those who are rid of sinful things It is hard to shrug those facts off The life is a full blueprint of Godliness Being liberated out of doldruns of sin And the drawbacks of its throes and thralldom Living in christ, breads and butters life Living in christ is heaven-on-earth A life that is commited to Christ Is doubtly sure of it's dreadfull wrong life of sin Twice as bothersome it makes amends to Christ A spick and span life whatsoever does not steel(harden) itself The recipe for it is righteousness at all time And nothing in life will cause a ripple Everything is a thing of beauty that thrills And walks down an avenue of peaces and relief

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Living In Christ is the author's thoughts on the joys of being a Christian.

Bible references: Colossians 1:27.

Heaven

High above the azure skies Is the land of the celestials Where the omnipresent, omniscience And the omnipotent Godhead stays A place where death ushers the saints to Who Lived godly lives of days True thought of the life that is to come Clothe the life that now is Christ standard is to be attained And that life after demise Is not moaned or is bemoaned The mind can't comprehend Our home unspeakable beauty It is a place of incomparable external As well as internal beauty Lo! Above it is the perfection of beauty Perfection of form colour sound and all A place of high holy ennobling companionship For the purest noblest, sinless fruit of earth Awaited by the founder high priest No breaking hearts for cheek glow ashen No heavy mind for eye glaze No watching the undertaker as he screws Down the coffin lid on the belove Bless me! no black dress and veils No funerals no dirge no grave No standing by a yawning grave Watching a coffin lowered into it No listening to the cold clods Falling remorselessly onto it The journey to this celestial city Starts now with breath on and soul propensity Agog, aware of where to rest On wither home, Heaven or Hell? Behold at anytime of the year Over and past is satan's gloomy reign The happy time of hallelujah songs is near When we are claded with christ glorious might When times ceases, when earth and sky Rings in memory lane of days gone by We rejoice together still from age to age Acclaiming our heavenly sacred heritage Eternally established above is christ throne He is the builder and the architect alone And the angels and saints shall intone Christ owns praises and blessings only.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Our hope is to be with Him and to be like Him.

Bible references: John 14:2–3.

The Unique Christ

Unique in every sense of the term He was born contrary to the law of life His death is contrary to the law of death He lived in poverty Poorer than a church rat He posses neither wealth Nor any social prestige But was content and celebrated For his remarkable disposition "In infancy He startled a king" In childhood he puzled doctors In manhood he ruled the course of nature He walked upon the waves as pavements And hushed the sea to sleep He had no bakery or fisheries But he spread a table for five thousand And have bread and fish to spare He healed the multitude without medicine And made no charge for his service He wrote no books but books are written of him He wrote no songs but songs are written of him He never practiced psychiatry Yet he has healed more broken hearts When he died few men put on sack cloths But a black crepe was hung over the sun Thou men trembled not for their sins The earth beneath them shook under the load And all nature honoured Him Corruption could not get hold of His body The soil reddened with His blood Could not claim His dust Once each week the wheels of commerce Cease their turning and multitude Wend their way to worship. Assemblies to pay homage to Him Though time has fleeted the conscience Of His crucifixion Yet He still lives Herod couldn't destroy Him, grave couldn't hold Him Now He stands forth upon the highest Pinnacle of heavenly glory Proclaimed of God Acknowledged by angels Adored by saints and feared by devils Give your life to this unique Christ Who has crossed the world's horizon? And you can while blissfully the rest of your days.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

The Unique Christ is God's promise fulfilled by reason of Christ's death and birth.

Bible references: Genesis 3:15.

Truth

Truth is not an esoteric philosophy Meant for the stoic philosophers In as much as He is invisible The lips of the truthful shall be establish He proves to the hilt and brings to book All evil deeds and motives With His potent and vocal force Truth is the rock of ages He stands without a tremble While raging waves pounds on, Only to be smashed to pieces He laugh at all attempt to discredit Him To those that are not kindly disposed to Him He lies both in words and relationship He is an impenetrable citadel to His adherent The bible is an epitome of Him Don't doubt, quibble or deny Him For the aftermath is very perilous Lie is devil-born and sin-coddled So dump it all at His throne of grace For He is so over reign He acts and that settled it He speaks and that is the final He is timeless forever true! He may be castigated, maybe conceited May be condemned, He is always true The mighty matchless message Meant for this day and age With Him heaven as your hope Shall become heaven home.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

Personification of truth.

Bible references: John 8:32.

His Way To Glory

Gentled, riding on a donkey His way to glory was met with praise Not with applause of parade or support of fame He rode on, the king of kings Without jeweled crown Tranquilly and modestly on a tiny creature As the embodiment of humility, His nature Slowly He move for the feeble and little to keep pace Gentle He is to all kind of race His voice open, receive and welcoming All and sundry with love unrejecting Heart broken guilt Find it natural to come to Him Walking with Him riding to glory They come confessing, He forgives They come weeping He consoles And becomes near Him as His own He rode on, heading not for coronation But for the green hill of gethsemane To Golgotha and the cross mainly The children with joyous mood cheers As their elders panicking heart fear At Golgotha spectators becomes serious seeker As His life seeds new life to all His way to glory was through death As seeds to ground and become new life Bringing forth many of it kind as fruit So all that comes to Him are His fruit Seeing that by law sin end with death His, was not martyrdom, but an act of atonement Which bring eternal life to His fruits.

— By Igbinijesu John

Summary

His Way To Glory is a poem that explains that "every star has a scar".

Bible references: John 20:25.
